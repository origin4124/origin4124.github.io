Episode 10 — What to do when you feel embarrassed that the same correction keeps coming back

A short founder-voice companion surface about a familiar training problem: the same correction can stop feeling like useful information and start feeling exposing when a dancer begins reading too much meaning into why the note is still returning.

← Back to Start Here

Episode note

Sometimes a repeated correction hurts more than a new one. Not because the note is harsher, but because it is familiar.

You hear it again and immediately something tightens. You think: I should already have fixed this. And from there, the correction can stop feeling like information. It starts feeling like exposure.

That is one of the moments this work is trying to support. Not by pretending repeated notes are easy. Not by flattening the embarrassment. And not by reducing everything to generic confidence language.

Just by slowing the meaning down. A repeated correction can be frustrating and exposing. But that is different from proof that you are failing or stuck in some final way.

The steadier move is to notice when the note starts feeling shame-heavy, separate the discomfort from the interpretation, and return to the actual task inside the correction so it can stay usable.
