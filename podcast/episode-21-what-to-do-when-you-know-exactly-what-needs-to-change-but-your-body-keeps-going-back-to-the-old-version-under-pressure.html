Episode 21 — What to do when you know exactly what needs to change, but your body keeps going back to the old version under pressure

A short founder-voice companion surface about a familiar training problem: sometimes a dancer understands the correction clearly, knows exactly what should change, and still feels the body snap back to the older version once pressure, timing, or speed return.

← Back to Start Here

Episode note

Sometimes one of the hardest moments in training is not confusion. It is knowing exactly what needs to change and still feeling your body go back to the old version anyway.

You understand the correction. You may even be able to feel the difference between the older version and the better one. But when the combination starts again and the pressure comes back, the body reaches for what is more automatic.

That can feel especially discouraging because the understanding is real. The correction did land. And that is exactly why the fallback can feel so personal.

This is one of the places where dancers need something steadier than shame. Knowing the correction consciously and holding it physically under pressure are not always the same stage of learning.

The steadier move is to let the clarity still count, even while recognizing that the body may need more repetition, pressure-testing, and time before the newer pattern becomes the one it reaches for automatically.
