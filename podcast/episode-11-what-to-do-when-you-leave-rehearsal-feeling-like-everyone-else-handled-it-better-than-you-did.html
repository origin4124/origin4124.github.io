Episode 11 — What to do when you leave rehearsal feeling like everyone else handled it better than you did

A short founder-voice companion surface about a familiar training problem: rehearsal can end with a dancer carrying not only their own rough moments, but also a story that everyone else adapted faster, looked steadier, or handled the room better.

← Back to Start Here

Episode note

Sometimes the hardest part of rehearsal is not only what happened in the room. It is what you start deciding about the room after you leave it.

You replay your own rough moments. You remember where you hesitated, where the pressure felt visible, where you wanted to look calmer than you did. And then the whole rehearsal starts collapsing into one conclusion: everyone else handled it better than I did.

That is one of the moments this work is trying to support. Not by pretending rehearsal pressure is imaginary. Not by flattening the comparison. And not by reducing everything to generic confidence language.

Just by slowing the story down. Rehearsal may really have felt hard. But that is different from actually knowing that everyone else handled the room better.

The steadier move is to separate what you actually know from the comparison story that starts filling in the blanks, so you can leave with a clearer next step instead of a heavier verdict.
