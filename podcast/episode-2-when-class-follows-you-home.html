Episode note

There are class days that stay in your body and your mind long after they are over.

You may be finished with class, but one correction keeps replaying. One combination keeps looping in your head. Or the whole emotional tone of the day is still sitting on your shoulders hours later.

That kind of day can be hard to leave behind. Not because the correction was wrong. Not because difficult training is unusual. But because sometimes there is no clear path between what happened in class and what you actually want to carry forward from it.

That is one of the reasons this support path is being shaped the way it is: not to make a hard day disappear, but to give it a better exit ramp. A way to name what happened, notice what was useful, and leave with one next step instead of carrying the whole emotional weight of the day around with you.
